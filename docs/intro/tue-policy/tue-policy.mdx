# TU/e RDM Framework Policy

## Research Data Management Framework Policy

The TU/e Research Data Framework Policy (RDM Framework Policy) defines roles and responsibilitiesfor researchers and research support staff regarding the management of research data.

### What Framework Policy means to you?

#### Data Management Plans (DMPs)

##### For PhD Candidates

A Data Management Plan is required for all research projects starting from September 2025. The DMP must be added to the Hora Finita system in the following 2 stages of the PhD:
Approved DMP at the 9-month evaluation
Approved DMP in the PhD defense package

##### For Research Staff (other than PhD)

A Data Management Plan is required for all research projects starting from September 2025.

##### For BSc, MSc, and EngD

A Data Management Plan is only required for research projects that include data from humans.

##### Ethical Approval

Ethical review is mandatory for all research involving human participants and/or processing personal data. See the Ethical Review Board (ERB) for more information.

##### Secure Data Storage

All research data generated or processed at TU/e must be stored in university-provided solutions (central or departmental/research group storage).

##### Data Agreements

Mandatory data agreements for (personal or IP-protected) data sharing with external parties.

##### FAIR Data

Final research data must follow the FAIR (Findable, Accessible, Interoperable, and Reusable) principles and deposited in a data repository, where required.

##### Data Package

Research data and documentation from published peer-reviewed articles must be archived in a data package.

### Practical implementation of the Policy

In order to comply with this Policy, we have developed [The Research Cockpit](https://cockpit.research.tue.nl/helpcenter/research/), where you can make your data management plan, ethical application, data sharing agreeemnt, FAIR data guidelines, and your data package.
